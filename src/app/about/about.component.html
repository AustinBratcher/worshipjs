Worship as Poetry

Worship is not linear. Worship is not mathematical. Worship is not a formula. Worship is not one size fits all.

Worship is a craft. Worship is a discipline. Worship is forming. Worship is informing. Worship is a call.

While the leader might have an idea of what worship should look like, he or she can not decide exactly what each and every participant will take away. They can only set an aim and hope their message is received with as much intention as it was crafted with. Worship is a place where conversation with God can happen. As with any good conversation, there is no real beginning or end. It is something we step into and out of. It is something our brothers and sisters and Christ are stepping into and out of as well. Worship is a place were we all come as a community to be with God and learn how God encounters the unique experiences we each have.

So what is this?

This website consitutes the "digital equivalent" of a worship service. While the role of technology in a worship service is a fruitful and intruiging conversation to have, this website is not commentary on that. Rather, this website serves as an example of how our different settings and circumstances shape the way we see and encouter scripture. It is specifically designed to have almost unique experiences for every viewer. Yet at the same time, there are intentional similarities built in for all. In the same way, an indiviaul viewer can have widely different or comfortingly similar views of this website. I'll leave it to you to form your own opinions from here.

This website is intended to be an interactive experience. Look at it at different times of day, in different places, holding your phone in different ways and changing the various settings (I dare you to turn off all the switches). All the while, ponder and pray with the scripture on the screen.

The colors on the screen are composed of standard red, green, and blue (RGB) components. The switches below will turn on or off (default is on) their corresponding color. Each color repsents some aspect of human life that goes into the worship experience. As the page is loaded, information is gathered, and the varying color components are each modified according to the information gathered about their "aspects of human life."

The Red

The red coloring represents the "regular" aspects of worship. We tend to go to worship at the same time and in the same place. Yet, sometimes, even changing pews or the time of day we go to worship can change our perspective on the scripture of the day.

Red is modified by the current time of day (to the nearest hour), your GPS location, and the scripture of the day (from https://beta.ourmanna.com).

The Green

The green coloring represents the way we orient ourselves towards the scripture of the day. Sometimes changing our own posture towards the scripture can drastically change the way we recieve it.

Green is modified by the current deviced used to open up the website (i.e. the user agent), the current viewport orientation of the device (i.e. landscape or portrait), and the orientation of the device in 3D space (for mobile phones).

The Blue

Blue represents the things in life we cannot control. Sometimes there are things far beyond our grasp that deeply effect the way we live and worship (for better or for worse).

Blue is modified by the current weather (from openweathermap.org) and the current stock price of the Nasddaq composite fund (ticker: IXIC).
